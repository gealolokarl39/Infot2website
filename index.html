History & Background
❮ ❯

The art of banig weaving in Libertad is a revered tradition that stems from the bariw plant (Pandanus copelandii), which is indigenous to the area. For generations, locals have been creating handwoven mats known as banig, which are valued for their cooling properties and intricate designs. This craft has been transmitted through generations and is celebrated annually during the Banigan Festival (March 14â€“16), which honors the skill and livelihood of the weavers.
